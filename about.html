BESË Review is a student journal dedicated to global affairs, human dignity, and the ethical questions that shape public life. It seeks to publish writing that is serious, reflective, and grounded in real-world issues.

The journal takes its name from the Albanian concept of “BESË,” a principle of honour, trust, and moral responsibility. It reflects a commitment to truth, integrity, and the obligation to engage with the world thoughtfully.

BESË Review focuses on topics such as human rights, inequality, public policy, and global systems, aiming to give space to ideas that are often overlooked or underrepresented.

Founder

BESË Review was founded by Orea Kastrati, a 15-year-old student engaged in the study of global affairs, human rights, and the structures that shape public life. Her work centres on analytical writing, critical inquiry, and the examination of overlooked realities.

Her writing has received international recognition through the Harvard Crimson Global Essay Competition, where she was named a regional winner in the journalistic category for work addressing systemic inequality. She has further engaged with global issues through Model United Nations, academic study in international diplomacy, and early exposure to legal analysis.

BESË Review was established not as a personal platform, but as a space for serious student thought—bringing together perspectives that approach global issues with clarity, discipline, and responsibility.

Founder’s Statement

BESË Review was created in response to a gap—not of information, but of attention. It is not a platform for opinion, but a space for disciplined inquiry into the realities that are too often overlooked.

Grounded in the principle of BESË—truth, integrity, and responsibility—the journal invites contributors who approach global issues with clarity, precision, and intellectual seriousness.
